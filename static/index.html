Global Vulnerabilities to Urban Transmission of Yellow Fever Virus

In 2016, the first case of yellow fever, a potentially deadly viral illness transmitted by infected mosquitoes, in Asia was reported. Meanwhile, four cases of yellow fever were imported into Europe over 8 months by travellers; before 2016, it took over 27 years. Climate change, rapid urbanization and international air travel, together may accelerate yellow fever transmission to new areas.

Where are Travellers Arriving From?

In 2016 alone, over 20 million travelled from yellow fever endemic areas to yellow fever endemic or suitable areas (each dot represents 10,000 travellers). Endemic areas are areas where the transmission of virus is naturally sustained; suitable areas are areas where conditions support virus transmission but no locally acquired cases have been reported.

Proof of Vaccination

0.1 million went to endemic cities in countries that do not require vaccination proof. Around 7 million went to suitable cities in countries that do not require proof of vaccination.

Several of the yellow fever suitable cities have some of the largest populations and receive the highest traveller volumes from endemic areas. The lack of vaccination policy is posing a threat to these cities.
